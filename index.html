Welcome to MPPT Solution

The MPPT-solution project aims to develop and demonstrate an open-source, low cost, and easily adaptable hardware design for tracking the maximum power output of novel PV devices, thus paving the way to a commercial first-of-a-kind electronic hardware for testing such PV technologies.

Project Objectives

The overall target of the project is to achieve affordable, flexible electronics for testing highly hysteretic laboratory PV samples that could be made accessible to a wide number of laboratories active in the field. This will be realized through the creation of a novel electronics board design that will be prototyped and demonstrated in laboratory application scenarios. The development of appropriate testing methods and equipment will permit reliable and accurate characterization of device performance, in controlled indoor and outdoor laboratory conditions, thus supporting the development and commercialization of next-generation PV technology such as perovskites.

  • To develop easily adaptable MPPT electronics for highly hysteretic laboratory PV.
  • To achieve low-cost electronics for testing highly hysteretic PV such as perovskite PV.
  • Evaluation of MPP tracking methods for testing hysteretic PV materials
  • Validation of the developed MPPT hardware using novel perovskite modules
