About the Project

UCY is the only organization participating in this project through the Degradation Laboratory (UCY-DegradationLab). UCY is the largest University in Cyprus, and it is ranked in the top 401-500 Universities worldwide (THE rankings). It has significant scientific expertise in PV with an excellent track record in research in the field of solar energy in the past 16 years. The UCY DegradationLab is a newly established strategic infrastructure unit at the University of Cyprus, which is an integral part of the Department of Electrical Engineering and FOSS Research Centre for Sustainable Energy. The DegradationLab focuses on studying perovskite-based photovoltaics particularly from the aspects of performance degradation, long-term stability and reliability as well as from the perspective of developing testing strategies and protocols for this new solar cell technology.

Infrastructure: The UCY-DegradationLab will make available for the project all its advanced testing infrastructure which includes both indoor testing facilities (namely a Class AAA steady-state solar simulator, a high resolution spectral response system, an EL/PL system, a Dark Lock-In Thermography and Illuminated Lock-In Thermography system, an automated Light-beam induced current system) and outdoor testing capabilities (namely a Keithley power source (for sequential I V), a number of environmental/irradiance sensors, dataloggers, and a LabView software for recording the IV traces at regular time intervals). The DegradatioonLab will also make available its 3D printing equipment for the MPPT prototyping. This infrastructure, particularly the outdoor test infrastructure, is highly relevant to the project and will be used for the validation and testing of the new MPPT solution developed in the project.
