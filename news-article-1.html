Πανεπιστήμιο Κύπρου: Έναρξη νέου ερευνητικού έργου proof-of-concept «MPPT-Solution»

May 19, 2026 By Andreas Polydorides 3 min read

The Degradation Lab of the University of Cyprus and the Photovoltaic Technology Laboratory of the University of Cyprus announce the launch of a new proof-of-concept research project entitled “MPPT-Solution” (project title: “Proof of concept of open-source, low cost, and adaptable MPP tracking system for perovskite PV devices”), which focuses on the development of economical and adaptable electronic systems for Maximum Power Point Tracking (MPPT) in new generation photovoltaic (PV) devices, such as perovskite-based photovoltaics. The official start date of the MPPT-Solution project was March 1, 2026 and its total duration is scheduled for 9 months.

The MPPT-Solution project aims to develop and demonstrate an open-source, low-cost and flexible hardware design for monitoring the maximum power point of innovative photovoltaic devices, which in most cases exhibit strong hysteresis in measurements. This will be achieved through the creation of an innovative electronic board design, which will be manufactured and studied under different laboratory conditions at the Photovoltaic Technology Laboratory of the University of Cyprus. The study will also include tests in real outdoor conditions, with simultaneous measurements of innovative photovoltaic devices operating under different MPPT algorithms. The final validation tests are expected to be carried out on large-area perovskite-based photovoltaic panels (in collaboration with companies abroad), collecting extensive performance data under outdoor conditions.

Essentially, through the demonstration of a proof of concept for low-cost MPPT electronics, the project aims to pave the way for the commercial exploitation of pioneering electronic hardware for the study of new photovoltaic technologies.
